Our Story

The four founders of this program, Shreya Musini, Padma Lakshminarasimhan, Pooja Mehta, and Trisha Jaishankar are rising seniors from Pleasanton, CA. In light of Covid-19, we were inspired to provide support for adolescents like us because there is a lack of focus on the emotional struggles teens are currently facing. Many teens/children are taking on adult responsibilities and are deprived of their daily norms to follow set safety regulations. Kids are being forced to balance various social, economic, and academic problems. Teens are facing mental health issues because we are being deprived of our usual interactions. Loneliness is the number one cause of suicide in teenagers. A recent study by Brigham Young University found that the loneliness teens have been facing can kill the same as smoking 15 cigarettes a day. Due to this pandemic, the number of loneliness related suicides will rise. Our lives revolve around electronic devices and are causing us to lose face to face interactions we have with others. The change from seeing our peers in school, 7 hours a day, to suddenly self-isolating ourselves has taken an immense toll on our mental health. To combat this, we want to provide awareness and emphasis on the mental health of children and teen by providing a safe medium to find help and interact with others facing similar struggles.
